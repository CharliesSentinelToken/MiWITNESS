Your Rights under UK Law

MiWITNESS helps explain your fundamental legal rights in everyday language.

Key Rights & Protections

  • Right to Fair Treatment: All public authorities must obey the law and treat you fairly under the Human Rights Act 1998.
  • Right to Complain: You can complain if you are mistreated by police, council, NHS, or other authorities.
  • Right to Evidence: You can ask to see the evidence held about you. This is called a Subject Access Request (SAR).
  • Protection from Retaliation: It is unlawful for authorities to retaliate if you complain or act as a witness (“whistleblower protection”).
  • Right to Compensation: If your rights are violated, you may be entitled to compensation or redress.

How to Make a Legal Complaint

  1. Describe what happened and who was involved.
  2. Include dates, places, and any supporting evidence.
  3. State what outcome you want (e.g. apology, investigation, compensation).
  4. Send your complaint in writing to the relevant authority.
  5. Keep a copy for your records and as digital evidence.

What Counts as Evidence?

  • Emails, letters, and documents
  • Photos, audio, video recordings
  • Statements from witnesses
  • Official responses or records

Getting Compensation (CICA)

If you are a victim of crime, you may qualify for compensation from the Criminal Injuries Compensation Authority (CICA).
